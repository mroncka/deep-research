# Research Methodology

## Core Philosophy

This pipeline is built on three axioms:

1. **Append-only cognition** — Knowledge compounds. Every run adds delta, never replaces. The KG, tools registry, and systems thread are living documents that grow monotonically.

2. **Signal over noise** — The goal is net-new, structurally significant findings. Repeated confirmation of known signals is noise. A good run surfaces 3–8 genuine deltas.

3. **Three-substrate parallelism** — Every finding is simultaneously:
   - A **node/edge** in the knowledge graph (factual, typed)
   - An **entry** in the tools registry (cost, stack fit, security posture)
   - A **correlation** in the systems theory thread (emergence, dynamics, pattern)

---

## Research Domains

### Track A — Capability Frontier
AGI benchmarks, model releases, performance leaps, emergent behaviors. Focus: what can systems do now that they couldn't 30 days ago?

### Track B — Agentic Infrastructure
Orchestration frameworks, runtime environments, protocol standards (MCP, A2A, AG-UI), deployment patterns. Focus: what is the operational stack for production agents?

### Track C — Security & Threat Surface
CVEs, supply chain attacks, malicious skill campaigns, ClawHub threats, privilege escalation patterns. Focus: what is the current attack surface of deployed agentic systems?

### Track D — Governance & Policy
NIST standards, regulatory timelines, compliance certification milestones, institutional endorsements. Focus: what constraints are becoming mandatory, and when?

### Track E — Systems Theory Correlations
Emergence patterns, power-law distributions, phase transitions, multi-agent collective behavior. Focus: what underlying dynamics explain the surface-level phenomena in A–D?

### Track F — Hardware & Infrastructure
Silicon roadmaps, inference cost curves, edge AI trajectory, datacenter architecture. Focus: what physical constraints bound or unlock the capability trajectory?

---

## The Delta Extraction Principle

Each research run produces a **delta set** — only findings that are:
- Not already in the KG/tools/systems thread
- Structurally significant (change the model, not just update a detail)
- Actionable or predictive (implies a future state or decision)

Findings that merely confirm existing nodes are recorded as "signal depth" (confidence boost) but do not generate new content.

---

## Synthesis Discipline

### The Three-Move Pattern
For every significant finding:
1. **State the fact** — what happened, what was released, what was measured
2. **State the implication** — what this changes about the current model
3. **State the prediction** — what this implies for the next 30–90 days

### Assertion vs. Inference
Always distinguish:
- `[CONFIRMED]` — sourced, peer-reviewed, or from primary documentation
- `[INFERRED]` — derived from multiple confirmed signals (flag reasoning chain)
- `[HYPOTHETICAL]` — forward projection (flag as such, include confidence estimate)

---

## Cadence & Run Structure

| Cadence | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Every 30 min | Full sweep across all 6 tracks + synthesis + commit |
| Daily (18:00 CET) | Evening briefing digest of top N findings |
| Weekly | Methodology review — assess query coverage gaps, thesis drift |

---

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

| Anti-pattern | Why it fails |
|--------------|--------------|
| Over-confirming known signals | Inflates run volume, dilutes genuine delta |
| Single-source facts stated as confirmed | Provenance contamination |
| Thesis attachment | Filtering out contradicting signals |
| Recency bias | Weighting new > important |
| Framework churn tracking | Patch notes ≠ structural change |
